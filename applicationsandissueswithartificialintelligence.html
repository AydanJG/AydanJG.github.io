Applications and Issues with Artificial Intelligence

    Artificial Intelligence is simulated or replicated human intelligence within a computer. AI allows a computer to do things such as think and problem solve. AI is supposed to be able to accomplish goals or make decisions that would normally need an expert to answer. Artificial Intelligence can do things such as reduce human error in jobs, assist in repetitive tasks, work tirelessly and more efficiently than humans. Not everything about AI is great, however. There are many concerns when it comes to substituting AI for jobs normally done by humans. Some worries about the application of Artificial Intelligence are unemployment, bias, ethical concerns, and humans losing control of AI. Overall, the use and development of AI can be very beneficial to society and can improve the efficiency and workforce of many companies and individuals

    There are three different subsets of AI that are meant for different applications. These are machine learning, neural networks, and deep learning. Machine learning is when AI uses massive amounts of data to find patterns within the data (Hao). One of the major uses of machine learning currently is in the autonomous automotive industry. Autonomous cars are equipped with machine learning algorithms that help them with “localization in space and mapping, sensor fusion and scene comprehension, navigation and movement planning, evaluation of a driver’s state and recognition of a driver’s behavior patterns” (Haydin). By using machine learning, the car is able to detect situations and its surrounding area in order to drive more safely. Deep learning is a more advanced type of machine learning. Deep learning tries to “get closer to the real meaning of artificial intelligence” (Vikram) by mimicking the human brain. While machine learning uses algorithms to detect patterns or images, deep learning actually allows the computer to teach itself. Deep learning uses a neural network to “mimic the activity of the layers of neurons in the neocortex” (Vikram). This means that each layer of these neural networks will try to go deeper and deeper on a topic.

    Artificial intelligence is already being applied in real-world situations all around the world by companies like Google, Facebook, IBM, and more. The reason so many companies are interested in AI development is because of the many benefits it can bring to society. AI is able to do things like assist the disabled, automate everyday tasks, understand diseases, and more. One example of this is in the life sciences industry where deep learning is used “for drug discovery, understanding of disease progression and so on” (Vikram). Researchers at The Australian National University are using deep learning to “understand the progression of Parkinson's disease” (Vikram). From something that was merely science fiction 50 years ago, AI is now being used in both complex, and simple repetitive tasks.

  One of the most involved companies in developing AI is Google. Google has developed a software called “Brain.” Brain is a deep learning technology that is used in all kinds of Google platforms including their search engine, voice recognition, email, maps, photos, and more (Vikram). Google has already implemented this into Android phones and uses it in order to “recognize voice commands, translate foreign language street signs or notice boards into your chosen language" (Vikram). “DeepMind” is another successful deep learning project acquired by Google which proved that AI can really surpass human skills. Google used this software in order to make its “AlphaGo” program, which beat the global champion at a Chinese game called Go (Vikram). Artificial Intelligence also is used heavily in the medical field. Recently, a brain implant was invented that allows paralyzed people to write using their minds. While this may sound like Science Fiction at first, researchers installed “two brain chip implants into T5's motor cortex [the part of the brain that controls movement]” (Shah). This brain implant allowed the paralyzed person to write at speeds of around 18 words per minute, which is only slightly slower than the average adult of similar age can type on a smartphone (Shah). With the help of AI, technology is able to help people accomplish things that were never before possible.

  While Artificial Intelligence may seem like an amazing future for society, not everything about it is perfect. The use of AI raises many ethical questions such as bias and a potential increase in unemployment rates. Bias in AI is “an anomaly in the output of machine learning algorithms” (Kantarci). Bias in AI algorithms is usually caused by “prejudiced assumptions made during the algorithm development process or prejudices in the training data” (Kantarci). There are two different types of bias, cognitive bias, and bias due to lack of data. Cognitive bias is a bias that can knowingly or unknowingly be input into an AI algorithm by its designer. Bias can also be due to lack of data or incomplete data that may only represent a specific group, instead of the whole population. This will cause a bias towards that group of people or things compared to others (Kantarci). Amazon demonstrated AI bias in their job recruiting tool powered by AI. This AI algorithm was meant to decrease manual labor in viewing job resumes. While the idea was good, the implementation was flawed. Amazon revealed that their AI actually showed a bias against women in the job application process. This was because the data set used was based on Amazon’s workforce, which was 60% male (Kantarci). Another concern about AI is the resulting increase in unemployment. AI replaces many common jobs. Not only is AI faster and more reliable, but companies do not need to pay wages to machines. This makes the shift to AI an obvious solution for many companies. While this may sound great, it may have serious repercussions. Many people will end up losing their jobs, and opportunities for employment will become less available when AI begins to be used commonly (Bossmann).

  Ethical Issues with AI do not stop at just the impact of machines on humans however. A complicated debate on whether AI should have rights has emerged recently. As stated earlier, Artificial Intelligence is simulated or replicated human intelligence within a computer. When simulating human intelligence and actions, one question that emerges is: what distinguishes a human from a machine? According to Hugh McLachlan, “It doesn’t seem controversial to say that we shouldn’t slander dead people or wantonly destroy the planet so that future generations of unborn people are unable to enjoy it as we have. Both groups are due moral respect and consideration. They should be regarded as potential objects of our moral duties and potential recipients of our benevolence.” If the unborn are given moral respect and consideration even though they are neither real nor artificial, then why shouldn’t AI be treated the same? Humans are able to make decisions, have feelings, and have emotions. If AI becomes conscious, it may also gain human-like emotion and be impacted by the way it is treated. The US constitution grants every citizen the right to "Life, Liberty and the pursuit of Happiness", so why shouldn’t AI be granted the same right if it can feel the same emotions humans can? If an AI system gains consciousness and begins to be able to think like a human, it would presumably not be ethical to create such consciousness since creating it would “imply ethical obligations to a sentient being, e.g., not to harm it and not to end its existence by switching it off” (Müller). The final concern when it comes to AI is the idea of singularity. The idea of singularity is that “the trajectory of artificial intelligence reaches up to systems that have a human level of intelligence, then these systems would themselves have the ability to develop AI systems that surpass the human level of intelligence” (Müller). This means that eventually, the AI that we create will surpass human knowledge and intelligence, and humans will begin to lose control of it. The idea of singularity was first created before computers even existed, and continues to be an interesting topic in people’s minds even today. Many movies, books, and plays have been based on this idea, and with the exponential rate of AI development we have been having, singularity may even pose a serious threat (Müller). This hypothetical point in time has not yet been proven or disproven to be possible, therefore it remains a hypothetical question, yet is still a serious concern when it comes to the development of self-conscious AI.

  Artificial Intelligence offers a bright future for our society, yet it has its flaws. There are several moral and ethical problems that are raised when discussing the repercussions of AI. It may take some time for society to get around these flaws before Artificial Intelligence begins to be used very commonly. Despite this, many companies are already making use of this new technology to improve our everyday lives. AI offers new hope to many people with physical and mental disabilities and can save lives with its superior research and analytical capabilities.
